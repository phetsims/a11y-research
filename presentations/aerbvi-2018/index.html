Interactive Simulations help students learn

Interactive science simulations

  • Are more readily available
  • Help students explore & think about science
  • Do not require special equipment
  • Create a safe lab-like exploratory experience
  • Allow students to experiment & discover science like a scientist

PhET Interactive Simulations

  • Started in 2002
  • 170 science & math simulations
  • Over 50 in HTML5
  • Run 100 Million+ times/year
  • 92 languages
  • Flexible, exploratory learning tools
  • Used with a diverse age range
  • Free of cost & openly licensed

Balloons & Static Electricity (demo)

Designed to

  • Be engaging
  • Be dynamic
  • Be intuitive
  • Link represenations
  • Connect to the real world
  • Be fun & game-like

Designed for flexibility

Sims come with

  • Engaging visuals & minimal text
  • No instructions
  • No prescribed learning path
  • No prescribed curriculum level

Teachers can

  • Design activities based on their own learning objectives
  • Integrate the sim in different ways depending on the type of learning activity
  • Guide & scaffold students depending on objectives & activities

Use sims in diverse ways

  • During lectures
  • Clicker questions (engage, poll, evaluate)
  • Introductory exploration (introduce topic with a sim)
  • Guided exploration - ask students to drive
  • Use worksheets & tables to scaffold investigative activities

When using a PhET Sim

We recommend teachers

  • Start activities with open play
  • Avoid explicit instruction
  • Make learning objectives explicit and meaningful
  • Monitor & measure student learning
  • Reflect & refine facilitation techniques

Example worksheet questions

  1. What happens if you move the balloon onto the sweater? Fill in the table below.

  2. What happens if you move the balloon to the center of the screen and let go of it? What happens? Explain why in terms of charges.

Guided exploration example

  • Let students do the probing as teacher may ask opened ended or challenge questions that guide.

What about accessibility?

For A11y: Interview diverse users

  • Students & adults with vision impairments using screen reader software
  • Consultants with vision impairments & science expertise

Accessible design goals

  • As simple & flexibile (true PhET fashion)
  • Minimize long-term maintenance costs & overall complexity

Accessible design features

{{Figure with large icons from the website}}

  1. Alternative input
  2. Auditory descriptions (read aloud with screen reader software)
    • Simple
    • Dynamic
  3. Sonification

Challenges: Alternative input

Wide range of interactive sim objects

Challenges: Auditory description

  • Many changes occur at once
  • Providing pathways to connect descriptions in different contexts

Challenges: Sonification

  • Layering of sounds (users cannot easily decipher multiple simultaneous sounds)
  • Balancing sound with description
  • Dealing with competing preferences (students with and without vision impairments)

Accessibility design goals (??)

Regardless of challenge, strive for designs that

  • Provide efficient navigation
  • Are easy & intuitive to operate
  • Support simulation's learning goals
  • Allow for collaboration with peers using the same sim

Accessible simulations (Demo)

  • Bold focus highlights & robust interactions
  • Simle for orientation & guiding
  • Descriptive alerts during interaction describing what is happening
  • Dynamic descriptions of changing state information available on-demand
  • Mixture of sound effects (real sounds) & mapped sounds (sonification)
