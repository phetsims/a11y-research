Interactive Science Simulations

  • Are more readily available
  • Help students explore & think about science
  • Do not require special equipment
  • Create a safe lab-like exploratory experience
  • Allow students to experiment & discover science like a scientist

PhET Interactive Simulations

  • Started in 2002
  • 170 science & math simulations
  • Over 50 in HTML5
  • Run 100 Million+ times/year
  • 92 languages
  • Flexible, exploratory learning tools
  • Used with a diverse age range
  • Free of cost & openly licensed

Balloons & Static Electricity

Is an example of one of PhET's interactive sims.

Sims are designed to

  • Be engaging
  • Be Dynamic
  • Be Intuitive
  • Link represenations
  • Connect to the real world
  • Be fun & game-like

Designed for flexibility

Sims come with

  • Engaging visuals & minimal text
  • No instructions
  • No prescribed learning path
  • No prescribed curriculum level

Teachers can

  • Design activities based on their own learning objectives
  • Integrate the sim in different ways depending on the type of learning activity
  • Guide & scaffold students depending on objectives & activities
    • Start with open play Avoid explicit instruction Make learning objectives explicit and meaningful Monitor and measure student learning Reflect and refine facilitation techniques

Teachers enjoy this flexibility

Use sims in diverse ways

  • During lectures - what will happen if I release the balloon near the wall? (two views)
  • Clicker questions (poll, engage, evaluate) - what is the net charge of the balloon?
  • Introductory exploration -
  • Guided exploration - ask students to drive
  • Use worksheets & tables to scaffold group or individual activities

Many ways to use PhET Sims

To support your students, you'll need to know

  • What sim or sims a teacher will use
  • How the teacher plans to use the sim
  • What supporting materials (if any) the teacher will use

What about accessibility?

For A11y: Interview diverse users

  • Students & adults with vision impairments using screen reader software
  • Consultants with vision impairments & science expertise

Accessible design goals

  • As simple & flexibile (true PhET fashion)
  • Minimize long-term maintenance costs & overall complexity

Accessible design features

  • Aleternative input
  • Description
  • Sonification
