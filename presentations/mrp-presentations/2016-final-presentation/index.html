PhET Sims are Effective Learning Tools

Target User

Student with vision loss who uses a screen reader and a keyboard.

Problem

  • No description for visual representations
  • No keyboard access for interaction
  • A black box to screen readers

Design Challenge

  • Access: Descriptions of visual representations in a mode that is perceivable to the user.
  • Action: Buttons & interactions that are operable via the keyboard.
  • Agency: Descriptions & interactions are understandable & make sense when accessed in different ways.

Methods: Inclusive Iterative Evaluation

  • Recruited 12 screen reader users: 5 women and 7 men, spanning 19 to 61 years.
  • Hardware: Desktop PCs (2), Mac Air (2), Surface Pro 3 Tablet (1), Lenovo E541 (1), Lenovo T520 (6)
  • Browser & Screen readers: Chrome & JAWS 17 professional (1), IE11 & JAWS 17 Home (1), Safari & VoiceOver (2), Firefox & JAWS 17 Home (1), Firefox & JAWS 15 (1), Firefox & JAWS 17 demo (5), Firefox & NVDA (1)
  • Made changes to the prototype & descriptions in between interviews.
  • Analysed recordings to identify issues with the design & the descriptions.

Design Features: PDOM, Hotkeys & Descriptions

What Worked Well

  • Parallel DOM is very effective; supports listening, then interacting; it does this in many ways.
  • Custom interactions & hotkeys for Balloon were learnable.
  • 3 aspects of Descriptions: Scene Summary, timely dynamic descriptions of charge information, timely alerts of UI changes.

Challenges

  • Navigation cues
  • Hotkeys combinations were hard to find
  • Descriptions not always understood
  • Balloon is a complex element
  • Browser & screenreader inconsistencies

Description Strategy Framework Emerged

  • Static: do not change; form an outline of the sim.
  • Dynamic: describe changes in the scientific model; form the main content of the sim.
  • Interaction Alerts: strings that do not change, & are delivered dynamically to support interaction.

Discussion: Complex Design Space

  • Technical issues with standards & browsers
  • WASD Keys less intuitive than Arrow keys, but learnable, & Arrow keys will eventually work.
  • Interactive sims pose unique challenges to the design of descriptions.

Conclusions & Future Work

Inclusive Design

  • Focused on screen reader users
  • Created an inclusive tool using an inclusive process
  • Benefit students with dexterity impairments, teachers & other makers of highly interactive learning tools

Future Work

  • Complete descriptions
  • Provide help in more flexible and findable ways
  • Investigate where sonification
  • Apply our description framework to other sims
